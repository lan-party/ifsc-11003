Frequently Asked Questions

1. What is a Mars rover?

A Mars rover is a robotic vehicle designed to explore the surface of Mars, conduct experiments, and send data back to Earth.

2. Who builds and operates Mars rovers?

Mars rovers are built and operated by NASA, often in collaboration with universities and international partners.

3. How many Mars rovers has NASA sent?

NASA has successfully sent five rovers: Sojourner, Spirit, Opportunity, Curiosity, and Perseverance.

4. What was the first Mars rover?

The first Mars rover was Sojourner, which landed in 1997.

5. What is the goal of Mars rover missions?

The main goals are to study Mars’ geology, climate, and potential for past or present life.

6. Are any Mars rovers still active?

Yes, Curiosity and Perseverance are currently active on Mars.

7. How do rovers communicate with Earth?

Rovers send signals to orbiting spacecraft around Mars, which relay the data back to Earth.

8. How are Mars rovers powered?

Some rovers use solar panels, while others like Curiosity and Perseverance use nuclear-powered systems called RTGs (Radioisotope Thermoelectric Generators).

9. How long do Mars rovers last?

Rovers are designed for a few months to a few years, but some, like Opportunity, lasted much longer than expected.

10. What did the Opportunity rover discover?

Opportunity found strong evidence that liquid water once existed on Mars.

11. What is special about the Curiosity rover?

Curiosity is about the size of a car and carries advanced scientific instruments to study habitability.

12. What is Perseverance searching for?

Perseverance is searching for signs of ancient microbial life and collecting samples for possible return to Earth.

13. Does Perseverance have any additional technology?

Yes, it carries a small helicopter named Ingenuity, which demonstrated powered flight on another planet.

14. How do rovers move on Mars?

Rovers use wheels and motors to slowly navigate the rocky terrain, guided by commands from Earth.

15. Can rovers make decisions on their own?

Rovers have limited autonomy and can avoid obstacles, but major decisions are controlled by engineers on Earth.

16. Why is Mars explored with robots instead of humans?

Robots can withstand harsh conditions and are safer and less expensive than sending humans.

17. What challenges do Mars rovers face?

Extreme temperatures, dust storms, rocky terrain, and communication delays make missions difficult.

18. What happens when a rover stops working?

When a rover can no longer communicate or operate, its mission is officially ended.

19. How long does it take signals to travel between Earth and Mars?

Signals can take between 5 and 20 minutes one way, depending on the distance between the planets.

20. What’s next for Mars exploration?

Future missions aim to return samples to Earth and eventually send humans to Mars.

Robotic Explorers on the Red Planet

About

The Mars rover missions send mobile robots to explore the planet’s surface, analyze rocks and soil, and search for signs that Mars once had conditions suitable for life. They examine the planet’s climate and geology to understand how it changed over time, especially the role of water in its history. These rovers also test new technologies and collect samples that may one day be returned to Earth, helping prepare for future human exploration.
